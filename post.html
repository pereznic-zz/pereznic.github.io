A Brownian motion simulator

Brownian motion behaviour was first explained by biologist Robert Brown in the begining of the 19th century. Nowadays, the term groups a wide variety of statistical models that are used in many fields such as physics, economics and social dynamics (amongst many others). In this first app, we created a basic Brownian model were the value observed at a specific timepoint, is completely random and normally distributed. We also added the "drift" parameter which can be inlcuded to model a growth between timepoints.

Such models are often used to model stock prices, only by using the current price and the historic estimates of the growth rate and volatility (as a risk measure). In this link you will find the Shiny App that allows to play with the model parameters and explore its behaviour.
